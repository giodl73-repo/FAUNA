## Repeated rubric gaps

1. Temporal pressure clarity: actionable windows such as dusk, migration week,
   frost week, wet season, or winter shelter period.
2. Pressure severity threshold: distinguish background presence from
   decision-changing pressure.
3. Life-stage specificity: breeding, nesting, lactation, larvae, brood, denning,
   or juvenile vulnerability.
4. Interspecies chain clarity: pest-predator, carcass-scavenger, rodent-flea, or
   pollinator-crop chains.
5. Human-animal interface boundary: keep FAUNA on animal evidence while leaving
   rules, economics, maps, and interpretation downstream.
6. Spatial scale fit: den, store, field edge, corridor, ridge, river reach, or
   settlement edge.
7. Consumer validation readiness: fixture can be consumed by at least two repos
   without custom interpretation.
